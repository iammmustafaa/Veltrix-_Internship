Color Combinations:
Color combinations refer to how different colors are selected and used together in a design to make it visually appealing.
Choosing the right combination helps create balance and improves readability. 
Common types of color combinations include complementary colors (opposite on the color wheel), analogous colors (next to each other), and monochromatic schemes (different shades of one color).
A good combination makes a design look clean, attractive, and professional.
Psychology of Colors:
Colors have a strong impact on human emotions and behavior. 
Different colors can create different feelings and meanings. 
For example, blue is often associated with trust and calmness, red represents energy and urgency, while green is linked to nature and growth.
Designers use color psychology to influence how people feel about a brand or message. Using the right colors helps in communicating the message more effectively.
